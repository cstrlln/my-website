Carlos Castrillon, PhD

Instructor

Department of Medicine, Division of Rheumatology · Emory University School of Medicine · Atlanta, GA

I am a junior faculty scientist working on basic and translational research of human B cells and Plasma cells. My work incorporates extensive training in wet- and dry-lab multiomics to answer fundamental questions in human immunology with therapeutic implications. I am establishing my research program in autoimmune diseases under the mentorship of Dr. Iñaki Sanz , and in plasma cell biology under the mentorship of Dr. Frances Eun-Hyung Lee. Through a career development award from the Lupus Research Alliance I'm studying the modulation of BCR signaling in systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE). I am also investigating mRNA technology to modulate and edit primary cells as potential therapeutics in autoimmune disease, or to enhance and sustain antibody responses after vaccination. I am particularly interested in exploring mRNA — in primary human cells specifically — not only as a therapeutic modality but as a platform tool for basic research and early-stage drug discovery.
